1 Name of the program 
The name of the program shall be “Neradi Eliya Samuga Atharavu Karam”, in short referred as NESAm and will 
be treated as an Alumni Welfare Program launched by IRTT Alumni Association. 
2 Brief introduction to the program 
➢ In an unfortunate event when a colleague or a friend of ours passes away, we go to their house to pay 
our last respect and console their family members. Typically, we go there along with a garland. 
➢ As part of NESAm, we collect a small amount as a Death Fraternity Contribution (DFC) from the NESAm 
Registered Members, for demise of each of the NESAm Member. 
➢ NESAm program pools the money thus collected and gives it to the family of the deceased alumni for 
immediate relief and in a sense saying indirectly “We care!” 
➢ No other program in the world can give such a huge benefit to the deceased NESAm Member’s Family 
with such a low contribution. When the bread winner of the family passes away, the family will be in 
deep trouble arranging funds immediately. NESAm supports the family of the deceased in a short span 
of time, particularly when the family needs financial support.

4 Byelaws of the Program 
This governing body will follow the prevailing byelaws of the Institute of Road and Transport Technology 
Alumni Association (IRTTAA). 
5 Aims and Objective of the Program 
1. To provide immediate substantial financial aid to the bereaved family of the NESAm member on 
his/her demise. 
2. To promote Life/ Patron Membership of IRTTAA. 
3. To promote Alumni Welfare Activities of IRTTAA.

7 Advantages of NESAm over other Term/ General Insurance Programs 
1. There is no need to pay a regular premium every year. Regular insurance policies require the insured 
person/ policy holder to pay premiums regularly every year, even if the policy holder does not have 
any need to use the insurance (meaning the policy holder and the dependents of the insurance policy 
is hale and healthy). 
2. DFC contribution for each NESAm member demise is very minimal compared to any other insurance 
program available in the open market. 
3. The NESAm program will give an Incomparable Satisfaction of helping the family of a deceased IRTT 
alumni who is also a NESAm Member. 
4. Patron/ Life Members of IRTTAA up to the age of 60 can join the program.

8 Terms & Conditions 
8.1 Activation plan for the Program 
1. The program will be “soft-launched” on 16th Feb 2025 at 36th Global Alumni Event at Government 
Engineering College, Erode. 
2. The registration along with applicable fees will be accepted from the date of soft launch. 
3. A minimum of 100 registered members are required to activate this Program within a maximum of 3 
months from the date of soft launch of the program. 
4. The program will be considered activated on the same day when the number of registered members 
reaches 100 and does not wait till the maximum 3-month period indicated. 
8.2 Eligibility Criteria 
1. Must be a Life/ Patron Member of IRTTAA. 
2. If both the husband and wife are alumni of IRTT and a member of IRTTAA, and intend to join this 
Program, they must join as individual members. The same is applicable for siblings or any other kind 
of relationship between two alumni. 
3. The member must not be 60 years of age as on the date of payment of the applicable membership 
fees for enrolment in the Program. 
8.3 Founding Member and Benefits 
1. Anyone registering for the NESAm Program in the first 3 months from the soft launch date is 
considered as “Founding Member”. 
2. A discount of 10% will be applied on the one-time membership fee paid during enrolment. 
3. The applicable one-time membership fee slab will be identified based on the year of graduation from 
the college and not based on the current age on the day of registration. 
4. The Death Fraternity Contribution (DFC) will be collected from the Founding Members for a maximum 
of 20 years or until they reach the age of 65, whichever is earlier. 
5. Founding members need not pay the DFC after this defined period, still they can enjoy the 100% 
benefits of the NESAm Program. 
8.4 Patron Member Benefits 
1. The alumni should be Patron Member on or before the day of registering for the NESAm program to 
avail the benefits for Patron Members. 
2. All Patron Members of IRTTAA are eligible for a discount of 5% on the one-time membership fee paid 
during enrolment. 
3. If a Patron Member is also a Founding Member, then a total discount of 15% is applicable on the one-
time membership fee.

8.5 Dispute Resolution 
In case of any dispute, the legal jurisdiction of the NESAm shall be at Erode or wherever the NESAm Office is 
functioning. 
8.6 Discontinuation terms for the Program 
8.6.1 Before the program is activated 
1. In the event of not achieving the minimum member count, the program will not be activated and any 
fees collected in this regard will be refunded in full to all the registered members, without any accrued 
interest. 
2. The accrued interest, if any, will be transferred to the Corpus Fund of IRTTAA and utilized for purposes 
that the fund is meant for. 
8.6.2 After the program was activated 
1. In case it becomes impossible to achieve the objective of the program, the same may be dissolved by 
passing a resolution in a General Body Meeting or in an Extra-Ordinary General Body Meeting of 
IRTTAA. 
2. The decision to dissolve this program may also be proposed when the active NESAm Member count 
falls below 100 and attempts to rebuild the member base do not yield result in an acceptable time 
frame of 6 months. 
3. In the event of passing a resolution to dissolve this program is passed, then in the following General 
Body Meeting or Extra-Ordinary General Body Meeting shall decide the final remaining amount 
related to the NESAm Program, after meeting the liabilities and debts, recovery of any assets. 
4. Following the acceptance of the final remaining amount with the NESAm Program, the same will be 
transferred to the Corpus Fund of IRTTAA and utilized for purposes that the fund is meant for. 
5. The decision taken in the General Body Meeting/ Extra-Ordinary General Body Meeting in this regard 
shall be final, legal and non-negotiable.

10 Membership Fees 
1. A one-time membership fee is collected at the time of registration, which is non-refundable once the 
program is activated. 
2. The corpus collected under this head will be used to manage the operational costs of the program like 
salaries, software subscriptions and so on. 
3. No additional funds will be collected from the members for managing the operational costs, without 
tabling the same in a General Body Meeting or an Extra-Ordinary General Body Meeting and passed 
as a resolution. 
4. The fees collected are based on the completed age of the alumni on the date of payment of this fee. 
5. The fees & the age range may be revised on a time-to-time basis, following a resolution in a General 
Body Meeting. The revised fees will not be retrospectively applied to the existing members and will 
be applicable only to the new members joining the program. 
6. To increase the member base of NESAm, promotional discounts may be offered on a time-to-time 
basis for a limited time on the one-time membership fees for the new members registering for this 
program. Such promotional offers are not applicable for existing members of this program and should 
not be seen as a discriminatory step taken by the NESAm committee to favour a single or a group of 
new members who will be joining the program.

Fraternity Contribution (DFC) in the event of demise of a NESAm Program Member.

2. Members will be informed about the fraternity contribution payment to be made by individual notice 
immediately after the demise of the NESAm Member, via the Mobile App notifications. 
3. The NESAm Members need to pay the DFC Contribution within 30 days from the notification through 
the Mobile App. 
4. The DFC slab will be based on the age of the contributing NESAm Member as on the date of the demise 
of the fellow NESAm Member for whom the DFC is being collected. 
5. The DFC will be collected from all members until the age of 70. Beyond this, while they are eligible for 
the benefit, they will not be contributing to the DFC. 
6. To enable ease of collection of the DFC, at the time of registration or on a periodic basis IRTTAA may 
require the members to issue standing instruction with their bank enabling deducting the DFC from 
their bank account as and when required. Any alternative way of collecting this fee may also be 
adopted by the committee if it is viable and limited to collecting the applicable DFC. 
11.1 Terms & Conditions 
1. During the time of registration, a refundable deposit is collected (5 times the DFC based on the age at 
the time of registration). 
2. If the DFC is not paid within 30 days, monthly reminders will be sent to the members, requesting them 
to make the payment at the earliest. 
3. The membership will be maintained in “active” state for a period of 1 year from the first instance of 
non-payment of DFC (after the 30-day window). This period is also referred to as the notice period for 
that member in the NESAm Program. 
4. If the member clears all the existing DFC dues within the notice period, the notice period will end, and 
the membership status will continue to be active. 
5. At the end of the notice period, if the DFC dues were not cleared, a notification will be given to the 
NESAm Member with total outstanding to be paid immediately, with a grace period of 15 days. 
6. At the end of the grace period, if the total outstanding was not cleared, then NESAm Membership of 
the alumni in the NESAm Program will be marked Inactive and the membership will be considered as 
lapsed. 
7. The refundable deposit collected during the registration shall be adjusted against the outstanding 
dues. 
8. In the event of any amount remaining in advance received after deducting the total outstanding DFC 
Dues, the same will be refunded to the NESAm Member’s account when written communication from 
the NESAm Member is received. Until a written communication is received from the NESAm Member 
in this regard, the amount will remain in the DFC Corpus in the NESAm Program. 
9. After the NESAm Membership is lapsed, if the alumni wish to re-join the NESAm Program later, they 
need to register in the program as a fresh applicant with the appropriate one-time non-refundable 
deposit to the NESAm Program, corresponding to their current age. 
10. The NESAm Program will be applicable to all the NESAm Members for their entire lifetime, as long as 
their membership is active by regularly paying the DFC dues without delay resulting in the lapsing of 
their membership, up to the age of 70. 
11. If an NESAm Member faces an unfortunate event 
a. During the notice period (until the membership is marked as Lapsed), the claim will be settled 
to the nominee after deducting the outstanding DFC dues by the NESAm Member.

12 Claim Settlement to the Nominee of the Deceased NESAm Member 
1. Claim settlement for any NESAm Member is only possible after the program is activated as described 
in section 8.1 Activation plan for the Program above 
2. The claim will not be processed for any cause other than the death of an active NESAm Member. 
3. After the program is activated, the claim for any deceased alumni will be considered only 3 months 
after the date of registration. This period is referred to as the dormant period. Exception may be 
considered if the cause of death of the NESAm Member is natural or accidental and not self-inflicted. 
4. The one-time claim settlement will be given to the nominee(s) of the deceased NESAm member, only 
when the deceased NESAm Member is active with no outstanding DFC dues or in notice period. If the 
membership was lapsed, the claim would not be processed. 
5. The claim form needs to be filled out with the details of the nominee(s), the bank account details along 
with the signature of the nominee(s). 
6. Refer to Annexure D: Illustration of possible one-time Claim Settlement amount which explains the 
total amount the nominee of the deceased NESAm Member will receiving as a one-time settlement 
from this program. 
12.1 Claim process 
1. The family members and nominees will be intimated that the deceased alumni have subscribed to the 
NESAm Program. 
2. The program benefits will be explained to them with the statistics of the active NESAm Member count. 
3. Their eligibility to claim the one-time settlement from the DFC of the existing NESAm Members will 
be explained. 
4. The family members will be requested to arrange the following information for record purposes of 
this program. 
a. IRTTAA Membership Card 
b. Attested Copy of the Death Certificate obtained from the competent authority. 
c. Requisition letter from the nominee(s) to sanction the amount along with the filled, signed 
death benefit claim form. 
d. All the documents needs to be sent to the official email id nesam@irttalumni.org, keeping 
secretary@irttalumni.org in copy. 
5. Once the request is received the claim will be processed and the DFC amount will be paid to the 
nominee(s). 
12.2 Beneficiaries 
1. The DFC will be given to the nominee(s) as per the latest records available in the profile of the 
deceased NESAm Member, which was collected during the registration or updated thereafter.

18 Annexure E: Comparison with Group Term Life Insurance 
An attempt is made to implement this program via the Group Term Life Insurance from leaders in the 
Insurance domain, instead of managing this by us by collecting DFC from all the members. 
18.1 Advantages 
1. The Sum Insured will be a fixed amount for all death claims. 
2. No overhead of collecting DFC from all members for the association. 
18.2 Disadvantages 
1. Most insurance providers do not support Group Term Life Insurance for an organization like ours, but 
only for organizations with Employer-Employee relationships. 
2. Cannot cover any Alumni living outside India. 
3. Those who support Group Term Life Insurance for organizations like ours want to cover the entire 
membership base and not a subset of members from the group. 
4. The first-year premium is high (in the tune of ₹ 4,000/- - ₹ 5,000/-) per person, and it will be revised 
with a minimum hike of 8%-10% on renewal, provided the claim ratio in the current year is less than 
100%. In the event of Claim Ratio being more than 100%, then the renewal premium will factor in this 
also, leading to a possible huge hike in the premium at the time of renewal. 
18.3 Conclusion 
After weighing in the advantages & disadvantages involved, it is decided to manage the NESAm by 
crowdfunding by collecting DFC from all members instead of opting for Group Term Life Insurance from any 
insurance provider.

NESAm – Frequently Asked Questions 
What is NESAm? 
NESAm (Neradi Eliya Samuga Atharavu Karam) is a community-driven financial support initiative 
designed to assist the families of deceased alumni through small contributions from fellow 
members. 
Why NESAm? 
➢ Incomparable satisfaction in helping fellow alumni families. 
➢ Minimal financial contribution for maximum impact. 
➢ No medical check-ups required. 
➢ Quick & simple claim process with minimal formalities. 
➢ Lifetime risk coverage with a one-time membership fee. 
➢ Can be availed by any Life/ Patron Member of IRTTAA, irrespective of their earning 
capability, resident status (NRI)/ citizenship (OCI, Foreign Citizenship) 
How It Works? 
➢ One-Time Membership Fee – Paid once during registration. 
➢ DFC Contributions – Minimal Death Fraternity Contribution (DFC) collected only when a 
fellow alumnus passes away. 
➢ Family Receives Support – Collected amount is handed over to the deceased member’s 
family. 
What are the eligibility criteria to be part of this program? 
➢ Must be a Life/Patron member of IRTT Alumni Association. 
➢ Maximum entry age is 60 years. 
➢ Each alumnus must register individually to be part of this program. 
What is the One-time Membership fee to be paid during registration?

At the time of enrolment, is there any other charges needs to be paid

What is the reason for collecting the membership fee?

➢ When a registered member passes away, the estimated DFC from all the members will 
be paid to the bereaved family from the corpus collected, which will eventually be 
refilled when the DFC is paid by all the members. 
➢ The interest generated out of the corpus will be used to meet the administrative 
expenses for this program like software subscription & maintenance charges, salary for 
the admin (if employed) and so on. 
Will my membership fee be refunded if I decide to opt-out of this 
program? 
Once the program is activated, the membership fee will not be refunded. Please remember that, 
once you opt-out of this program, to enrol again, the membership fee will need to be paid based 
on your completed age at the time of re-enrolment. 
Why should I enrol myself when I have coverage for my life via an 
individual Term Insurance policy? 
We get an immense satisfaction of supporting a fellow alumni’s family in time of a need by 
contributing a very minimal amount (DFC) in a time of need. 
My husband is a registered member of NESAm. Should I also register for 
this by paying the membership fee? 
Yes. Irrespective of your relationship with another alumni, you need to register yourself 
individually by paying the one-time membership fee and pay DFC regularly. 
In case of demise of the registered member, to whom the NESAm 
program benefit will be settled? 
It will be settled to the nominee(s) registered by the member of the program. Registration of one 
or more nominees is a mandatory requirement during enrolment. The details of the nominee can 
be updated anytime by the registered member. 
Payment will be made to the Legal Heir(s) (divided equally) in case all the assigned nominee(s) 
are not alive. For the legal heir(s) to claim this, they need to produce the legal heir certificate 
obtained from the competent authority. 
At the time of claim if my nominee is not alive, to whom the settlement 
will be made? 
The app allows updating of the nominee details at any time. In case if it is not updated, and the 
nominee is not alive, the DFC collected will be paid to the legal heirs as per the certificate issued 
by a competent authority. 
What happens to my membership if I do not pay the DFC regularly? 
As this is a community support scheme, you need to pay the DFC regularly within the stipulated 
time. If for any reason you are not able to pay the DFC on time, reminders will be sent to you via 
all possible communication modes for a period of 1 year, referred as notice period. After that a 
15-day grace period will be provided to clear all the DFC dues. 
After this grace period also if the dues are not cleared, your NESAm membership will be 
deactivated, and your life is not covered under this program. 
To become a member again you need to enrol yourself as a new member by paying the one-time 
membership fee, based on your completed age at that point in time. 
If my membership is deactivated, will my membership fee be refunded? 
No. If the membership is deactivated due to non-payment of DFC, the one-time membership fee 
will not be refunded. The balance in Advance DFC (if any) after deducting the DFC dues will be 
returned to you. 
Is there any special discount for Patron Members of Alumni 
Association? 
For Patron Members, a standard discount of 5% is provided on the one-time membership fee. 
There is no discount on DFC. 
Who is a Founding Member and what are the benefits of being one? 
By design, this program can make a huge impact when the member base registered for this is 
program is big. To encourage more enrolment when launching, anyone registering within 3 
months since launch are recognized as Founding Members. 
The benefits of being a Founding Members are: 
• 
10% discount on the one-time membership fee. 
• 
DFC is paid till their age of 65 (not until 70) or to a maximum of 20 years, whichever is 
earlier. 
• 
The membership fee is decided based on the age-band or the graduated batch which ever 
results in a lower amount. 
I am a Founding Member and a Patron Member. What is the discount I 
will get on my membership fee? 
As a Founding-Patron Member, you are eligible for a total discount of 15% on the one-time 
membership fee. 
What happens to this program if the member count is too low, resulting 
in the DFC collected is insignificant to support the bereaved family? 
This program will be activated only after it breaches the critical mass of 100 members. If the 
program could not breach the critical mass, then this program will not be activated, and the one-
time membership fee paid will be refunded. 
After activating the program if the member count falls below the critical mass, and efforts to 
increase the membership base fails, then the program will be discontinued, and the remaining 
corpus will be utilized for other welfare schemes as decided in a following AGM of IRTTAA. 
Will there be any special situations by which I can maintain my 
membership but not contribute to DFC? 
The bereaved family gets benefited only via the DFC contribution by every member registered in 
this program. Given the importance of the DFC, it is important for everyone to contribute the DFC 
in a timely fashion. 
However, if any reasonable context is brought to the notice where someone is mentally/ 
economically in a bad situation which prevented them from paying the DFC dues on time which 
resulted in the lapsing of membership, the claim from the nominee may be honoured under 
special circumstances. The decision of the IRTTAA Office Bearers and the NESAm Committee will 
be final and binding in evaluating whether the context is reasonable or not in this regard. 
Did you consider providing the program via standard insurance service 
providers? 
Yes, we approached many insurance providers for availing Group Term Insurance in the 
backend. Most insurance providers provide Group Term Insurance only for Employer-Employee 
based groups and not to a Alumni Group like ours. The few who came back with a proposal had 
the following shortfalls/ challenges: 
➢ The coverage/ premium will be for the entire member base of IRTTAA and not for a 
subset. 
➢ The members should be earning. Non-earning members are excluded from the 
coverage. 
➢ Should be residing within India, and NRIs will be excluded from the coverage. 
➢ The premium for a coverage of 5 lakhs was in tune of ₹ 65 Lakhs for the first year. The 
premium will be increased in the following year by a minimum of 8-10%. If our claim 
ratio is high, then the renewal premium will be impacted accordingly. 
➢ Details about the kind of work, CTC and Medical condition of everyone needs to be 
provided, and the first-year premium may be impacted once this detail is shared with 
them. 
➢ Every year the premium needs to be paid 
As our association does not have any other source of income other than the one-time 
membership fee for Life/ Patron Member, it is not feasible to fund the annual premium without 
collecting the same from all the members. Moreover, collecting the premium from all the 
members of IRTTAA is not possible at all. Hence, after evaluating the options, this program is 
designed to use “community funding” approach.

1. Introduction 
1.1 Purpose 
The purpose of this document is to define the functional and non-functional requirements for 
the NESAm software ecosystem. This system aims to digitize the operations of the alumni 
welfare program, which operates on a crowdfunding model known as "Death Fraternity 
Contribution" (DFC). 
1.2 Scope 
The software solution will consist of three primary components: 
1.​ Mobile Application (iOS & Android): For alumni members to register, pay fees, view 
status, and receive demise notifications. 
2.​ Web Admin Portal: For the NESAm Committee to manage members, verify documents, 
trigger demise events, calculate DFC collections, and process claims. 
3.​ Analytics & Dashboards: A suite of visualization tools for both members and the 
committee to track member statistics, growth trends, benefited families, and the overall 
social impact of the program. 
1.3 Key Definitions & Acronyms 
●​ DFC (Death Fraternity Contribution): Variable financial contribution collected from 
active members upon the demise of a fellow member. 
●​ Advance DFC (Security Deposit): A one-time refundable deposit collected during 
registration. Crucial: This amount remains untouched in the user's wallet as a security 
buffer and is not used for regular contribution payments. It is only utilized for adjusting 
dues upon Lapsing or Death Settlement. 
●​ Founding Member: An honorary status automatically assigned to any member 
registering within a specific window (e.g., 3 months) of the program launch. 
●​ Life Member: A registered member of the IRTTAA who has paid the applicable one-time 
membership fees. 
●​ Patron Member: A specific membership tier within the parent IRTTAA association, 
typically involving a higher contribution than a Life Member. 
●​ Dormant Period: A waiting period post-registration during which natural death claims 
are not honored. 
●​ Lapsed Member: A member who failed to pay DFC dues after the notice period and 
grace period. 
●​ User UUID: An immutable, system-generated unique identifier for a user profile, 
independent of their mobile number.

○​ The system must validate the user against the Parent Association Database

○​ Check: Is the user listed? 
○​ Retrieve Status: Is the user a Life Member or Patron Member? (This status is

3.3 Dynamic Fee Calculation Engine 
The system must automatically calculate the One-Time Membership Fee and Advance DFC 
based on the following logic: 
●​ FR-MA-04 (Age Basis): Fee calculation is based on "Completed Age" on the date of

●​ FR-MA-05 (Slab Optimization): 
○​ Regular Members: Fee is based strictly on the Age Slab active at the time of

○​ Patron & Founding Members: The system must compare the fee for the Age Slab 
against the Graduation Year Slab and apply the lower of the two. 
■​ Note: Graduation Year is captured from user input (validated) to perform this 
optimization. 
■​ Note: "Patron" status is derived from the lookup in FR-MA-01. 
●​ FR-MA-06 (Discounts): 
○​ Founding Member: Apply Founding_Discount_% (Automatic tag if joined within the 
configured "Founding Window"). 
○​ Patron Member: Apply Patron_Discount_% (Based on Parent DB status). 
○​ Founding Patron: Apply Founding_Patron_Discount_% (Cumulative). 
○​ Note: These percentages are configured in FR-WEB-17. 
●​ FR-MA-07 (Advance DFC Calculation): 
○​ The Advance DFC acts as a Security Deposit. 
○​ Formula: Advance DFC = (DFC Rate for User's Age) * (ADVANCE_DFC_MULTIPLIER). 
○​ The system must look up the DFC Rate (FR-WEB-16) and the Multiplier (FR-WEB-17) 
to perform this calculation dynamically at the time of payment.

○​ Self-Declaration Form (Annexure C).

○​ Total Families Benefited (Claims Settled). 
○​ Total Assistance Provided (Cumulative ₹).

3.8 Guest Access 
●​ FR-MA-18 (Public View): Guests must be able to view the "About NESAm" page,

●​ FR-MA-19 (Impact Teaser): Guests see anonymized counters of Total Families Benefited

3.9 Profile & Account Management 
●​ FR-MA-20 (Lapsed Member Renewal): 
○​ If a user logs in and their status is Lapsed, the dashboard shall disable "Pay Dues" 
and instead show a "Apply for New Membership" button. 
○​ This action triggers a new application flow. Fees are calculated based on current age 
and current Fee Slabs. 
○​ Data: The system retains the existing User Profile (UUID) but generates a new 
NESAm Membership ID. 
●​ FR-MA-21 (Profile Updates): 
○​ Authenticated users must be able to easily update non-critical profile fields. 
○​ Editable Fields: Residential Address, Email Address, Mobile Number, Nominee 
Details (Names, Relationships, Shares). 
○​ Verification: Updating the Mobile Number requires OTP verification of the new 
number only. 
○​ Read-Only Fields: Date of Birth, Graduation Year, Full Name, and Membership Type 
(Life/Patron). These fields are locked after initial verification as they determine 
financial liabilities. 
3.10 History & Receipts 
●​ FR-MA-22 (Transaction History): 
○​ The Mobile App must provide a "Passbook" or "History" view containing a 
chronological list of all financial transactions. 
○​ Credit Events (Inflow): Membership Fee Payments, DFC Payments (Dues Cleared). 
○​ Debit Events (Outflow): Invoice Generation (Demand Note created). 
○​ Receipt Generation: For every Credit Event (Payment made by user), the system 
must allow the user to download a PDF Receipt containing Transaction ID, Date, 
Amount, and NESAm branding. 
3.11 Notification Center 
●​ FR-MA-23 (Consolidated Inbox): 
○​ The Mobile App must have a "Notification Center" that displays a history of all alerts 
received by the member. 
○​ Intent-Based Grouping: The view must show the Intent of the message (e.g., 
"Payment Reminder", "Demise Alert", "Deposit Demand") rather than duplicate 
entries for every channel (SMS/Email/Push). 
○​ Read/Unread Status: Visual indicators for unread messages. 
○​ Retention: Notifications should be viewable for a minimum of 12 months. 
4. Functional Requirements: Web Admin Portal 
4.1 Member Onboarding & Verification 
●​ FR-WEB-01 (Verification Queue - Verifier): Interface for the Verifier to review pending 
applications, cross-check IRTTAA ID cards, and validate Self-Declarations. 
●​ FR-WEB-02 (Approval/Rejection): 
○​ Verifier marks documents as "Verified" or "Defective". 
○​ System automatically Approves membership once Payment is confirmed AND 
Documents are Verified. 
●​ FR-WEB-03 (Founding Tag): System automatically tags members as "Founding 
Members" if the application date is within the configured Founding Window (See 
FR-WEB-17). 
●​ FR-WEB-04 (Parent DB Management): Admin interface to upload/update the "Parent 
Association Database" (CSV/Excel import) used for lookup validation. 
4.2 "Demise Event" & Contribution Engine (Core Logic) 
●​ FR-WEB-05 (Event Trigger - Verifier): Form for Verifier to record a member's death. 
○​ Requires upload of Death Certificate. 
○​ Input: Deceased Member ID, Date of Death. 
●​ FR-WEB-06 (Global Demand Generation - System): Upon verification of a Demise 
Event, the system must generate a Payment Request (Invoice) for every other active 
member. 
○​ Logic: The amount (₹100 - ₹500) is determined by the Contributor's Age looking 
up the DFC Slab active on the Date of Demise. 
○​ Exemptions: 
■​ Senior Members: Members > 70 years old do not contribute. 
■​ Founding Members: Contribute only for 20 years or until age 65 (whichever is 
earlier). 
●​ FR-WEB-07 (Collection Oversight - Treasurer): The system tracks the status of these 
demands (Paid/Unpaid). The Treasurer monitors the collection progress via the 
dashboard. 
4.3 Membership Lifecycle Management 
●​ FR-WEB-08 (Notice Period Logic): 
○​ If a DFC Demand Invoice is unpaid after 30 days, status changes to Notice Period. 
○​ Notice Period lasts for 1 year. 
●​ FR-WEB-09 (Lapsing & Deposit Adjustment): 
○​ If unpaid after Notice Period + 15 Days Grace, status changes to Inactive/Lapsed. 
○​ Deposit Adjustment: The system automatically liquidates the member's Advance 
DFC (Security Deposit) to cover/write-off the pending dues. Any remaining balance is 
forfeited to the association corpus (or refunded based on admin discretion/bylaws). 
●​ FR-WEB-10 (Rejoining Workflow): 
○​ The system must allow a previously Lapsed user (identified by UUID) to initiate a New 
Application. 
○​ The system must enforce that a single User Account can have only one Active or 
Notice Period membership at a time. 
○​ Historical (Lapsed) membership records are archived for audit but not reactivated. 
4.4 Claims Processing 
●​ FR-WEB-11 (Claim Validation - Verifier): 
○​ Verifier checks: Is Program Activated? Is Deceased Member Active? Is Date of Death 
> Dormant Period? 
●​ FR-WEB-12 (Settlement Calculation & Adjustment): 
○​ Treasurer reviews the calculated payout sum (based on collected DFC). 
○​ Adjustment Logic: If the deceased member had any Pending Dues (e.g., died 
during Grace Period), the system must deduct these dues from the Final Settlement 
Amount. 
○​ Treasurer authorizes the net disbursement to the Nominee's Bank Account. 
●​ FR-WEB-23 (Settlement Record Generation): 
○​ Upon authorization, the system must lock the calculation into a permanent 
Settlement Record. 
○​ Formula: Gross_Collection - Pending_Dues = Net_Payout. 
○​ Output: A PDF Settlement Statement showing the breakdown, payment date, and 
transaction reference. 
4.5 Analytics & Reporting (Treasurer & Auditor) 
●​ FR-WEB-13 (Growth Trends): Interactive charts showing enrollment velocity and 
demographics. 
●​ FR-WEB-14 (Financial Health - Treasurer): 
○​ DFC Collection Efficiency (Paid vs. Pending). 
○​ Advance DFC Float (Total refundable deposits held). 
●​ FR-WEB-15 (Audit Logs - Auditor): 
○​ Read-only view of all manual overrides, verification actions, and financial 
disbursements. 
●​ FR-WEB-21 (Notification Audit Logs): 
○​ Admin View: Admins must be able to view a log of all notifications sent to a specific 
member or broadcast to all. 
○​ Channel Details: The log must explicitly list which channels were attempted and 
their gateway status (e.g., "Email: Sent, SMS: Failed, Push: Delivered"). 
○​ Filter: Ability to filter logs by Date Range, Member ID, or Notification Type (e.g., 
"Show all Lifecycle Warnings sent last week"). 
4.6 Configuration & Governance (Admin) 
●​ FR-WEB-16 (Temporal Configuration for Fees/Rates): 
○​ The Admin must be able to update Fee Slabs (Membership Fees) and DFC Rates. 
○​ Versioned Updates: Updates must not override historical data. The system must 
store slab configurations with a Valid_From and Valid_To date. 
○​ Application: 
■​ New Registrations: Use the Fee Slab where Valid_From <= Current_Date <= 
Valid_To. 
■​ Demise Events: Use the DFC Rate Slab where Valid_From <= Date_Of_Demise <= 
Valid_To. 
■​ Historical Reports: Must reference the slab that was active at the time of the 
transaction. 
●​ FR-WEB-17 (Configurable Program Parameters): 
○​ The following system variables must be configurable by the Admin (not hardcoded): 
■​ Program Launch Date: The reference date for the program start. 
■​ Founding Window Duration: (e.g., 90 days). Defines the cutoff for "Founding 
Member" status. 
■​ Advance DFC Multiplier: (e.g., 5). Defines the deposit calculation factor. 
■​ Dormant Period Duration: (e.g., 3 months). 
■​ Notice Period Duration: (e.g., 1 year). 
■​ Discount Rates (New): 
■​ Patron_Discount_% (e.g., 5%). 
■​ Founding_Discount_% (e.g., 10%). 
■​ Founding_Patron_Discount_% (e.g., 15%). 
4.7 Notification & Scheduler Engine 
●​ FR-WEB-18 (Demise Broadcast & Demand): 
○​ Upon Verification of a Demise Event, the system must broadcast notifications with 
Conditional Messaging based on member status: 
○​ Target A: Contributors (< 70 Years & Active): 
■​ Message: "We regret to inform you of the demise of [Name]. A payment request 
of [₹XXX] has been raised. Please pay via the app to support the family." 
○​ Target B: Exempt Members (> 70 Years): 
■​ Message: "We regret to inform you of the demise of [Name]. (No contribution is 
required as you are covered under the Senior Member Exemption)." 
●​ FR-WEB-19 (Automated Financial Scheduler): 
○​ The system shall run a daily background job to enforce financial health. 
○​ Task A: Outstanding Dues Check: Identify members with unpaid DFC demand 
invoices. 
■​ Send Gentle Reminders at T+15 and T+25 days. 
○​ Task B: Age Band Transition Check: 
■​ Identify members whose age has crossed into a higher DFC Rate Slab as of 
Current_Date. 
■​ Exemption Check: If New_Age > 70, set Required_Advance_DFC = 0. Do not 
send demand notices. 
■​ Else, Recalculate Required Advance DFC = New_Rate * Multiplier. 
■​ If Security_Deposit_Balance < Required_Advance_DFC: 
■​ Calculate Differential_Amount = Required - Security_Deposit. 
■​ Send Deposit Demand Notification: "Happy Birthday! Your DFC slab has 
changed. Please top up ₹XXX to maintain the required Security Deposit." 
●​ FR-WEB-20 (Lifecycle Critical Alerts): 
○​ Notice Period Warning: Sent at T+29 days (1 day before entering Notice Period). 
○​ Lapse Warning: Sent at T+350 days (15 days before permanent Lapsing). 
○​ Channel: SMS + Email + Push Notification (High Priority). 
●​ FR-WEB-22 (Policy Change Impact Processor): 
○​ Trigger: Initiated automatically when Admin updates ADVANCE_DFC_MULTIPLIER or 
DFC_Rates (FR-WEB-16/17). 
○​ Process: 
1.​ System batches all active members. 
2.​ Filter: Exclude Members where Age > 70 (They are exempt from Advance DFC 
obligations). 
3.​ Recalculates New_Required_Advance for remaining members based on their 
current age and the new parameters. 
4.​ Checks Current_Security_Deposit vs New_Required_Advance. 
5.​ If Shortfall > 0, queues a Deposit Demand Notification. 
○​ Message: "NESAm Policy Update: The Advance DFC requirement has changed. 
Please top up ₹XXX to align with the new norms." 
5. Non-Functional Requirements 
5.1 Security 
●​ NFR-01 (Data Privacy): PII (Personally Identifiable Information) and Nominee details 
must be encrypted at rest. 
●​ NFR-02 (Access Control): Strict enforcement of the Roles defined in Section 2. 
Auditors must never have write access. 
5.2 Performance & Reliability 
●​ NFR-03 (Batch Processing): The Contribution Engine must process DFC calculations for 
2,000+ members within 60 seconds of a Demise Event trigger. 
●​ NFR-04 (Availability): 99.9% uptime required for the DFC collection module. 
5.3 Audit & Compliance 
●​ NFR-05 (Financial Audit): Immutable logs for all "Advance DFC" deductions and 
"Membership Fee" collections, accessible to the Auditor. 
●​ NFR-06 (Refunds): Logic to handle refunds if the program fails to activate (Member 
count < 100 in 3 months).
